How the tax charge works for you or your partner

You or your partner’s income is between £50,100 and £60,000 in a year. The person with the highest income may have to pay a tax charge, called the High Income Child Benefit Charge.

If you or your partner choose to receive Child Benefit payments, the payments you (or your partner) may get will be more than the value of the tax charge. To pay the charge, the person that has the higher income must set up and fill in a Self-Assessment tax return each tax year.

Only one person can get Child Benefit for a child, so you need to decide if you or your partner should claim. If one of you is not paying National Insurance because they’re not working or they earn less than £242 per week, it might be better for that person to claim.
